Jellyfish

Jellyfish are marine animals that are shaped like umbrellas and have long tentacles flowing behind them.

Origins

The existence of jellyfish can be dated back to over 500 million years ago, potentially even 700 million years ago. Jellyfish are the earliest known animals to have organized tissues and a nervous system, although they donâ€™t have any bones. Their lack of bones makes it difficult to find early jellyfish fossils.

Habitat

Jellyfish are found in a variety of bodies of water around the world, as they can adapt easily. They are found in warm and cool oceans, coastlines, and deep waters. However, the most poisonous species of jellyfish are typically found in the warm waters of Southeast Asia and Australia.

Predators

Jellyfish have several natural predators, some of which include fish, turtles, birds, whales, and crabs. Specific predators include ocean sunfish, grey triggerfish, leatherback sea turtles, whale sharks, fulmars, arrow and hermit crabs, and humpbacks.

Diet

As there are over 2000 known species of jellyfish, their diets can vary. However, jellyfish are generally carnivorous animals and choose their prey based on their size. Smaller jellyfish tend to eat smaller sea creatures like plankton and crustaceans while larger jellyfish opt for larger sea creatures like fish and shrimp. Although jellyfish are carnivorous, they will consume plant matter or their own kind if food sources are scarce, as they are opportunistic predators.
